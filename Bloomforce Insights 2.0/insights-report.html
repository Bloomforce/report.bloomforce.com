Takeaway 01 · Leadership

Where you work barely matters — until you reach the top.

An Epic analyst earns about the same across most org types. Step into leadership and the picture splits wide open: academic medical centers carry a real discount that grows with every rung.

Takeaway 02 · Credentials

A clinical license resets the pay scale.

Willow (pharmacy) build roles that require a PharmD or RPh don’t pay like analyst roles — they pay like pharmacists who happen to build Epic.

Why it matters: a credential is worth roughly +29% (~$32k) on the same module. The same pattern shows up wherever a build role needs a clinical credential — RN for clinical modules, RHIA/RHIT for HIM.
Takeaway 03 · Work model

Remote isn’t a discount here — it’s a premium.

Conventional wisdom says remote roles trade pay for flexibility. In Epic build, the opposite holds: the work is national, and the remote market sets the price.

Takeaway 04 · The market is honest

What’s advertised and what people make line up — within ~1%.

Across Epic analyst roles, posted ranges and real reported pay sit within about a percent of each other. That’s rare, and it’s a sign of a mature, well-priced market.
